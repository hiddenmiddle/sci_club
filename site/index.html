// what we do

Deep reading, together

A small, serious club for anyone who wants to go deep into theoretical physics and mathematics. Every week we gather to read, discuss, prove, and code our way through carefully chosen books.

∫

Read

Deep engagement with books chosen for conceptual richness and mathematical beauty — from complex analysis to quantum field theory.

ℏ

Discuss

∇

Create

Code visualizations in Python, present proofs, build interactive demos, design problem sets. Understanding through doing.

Our first book

Visual Complex Functions

Elias Wegert — An Introduction with Phase Portraits

Our journey begins with one of the most visually stunning areas of mathematics. Phase portraits use color to reveal the hidden structure of complex functions — where zeros and poles live, how functions behave near singularities, and what makes analytic functions beautiful. The hue encodes the argument (phase) of the output, turning abstract algebra into vivid, intuitive images.

  • 7 chapters over approximately 2 months
  • Weekly meetings led by Alex
  • Reading, presenting proofs and key concepts
  • Coding our own phase portraits in Python
  • Dense but short — a perfect first sprint

Future explorations

These are books we're considering for future cycles. Nothing is set in stone — we'll decide together as a club.

Alex's pick

Abel's Theorem Through Problems

V.B. Alekseev

Group theory through problems, leading to one of the most fundamental results in mathematics. Complex numbers, Riemann surfaces, Galois groups — built from scratch through problem-solving.

~3 months · 352 problems

Vika's pick

QFT in a Nutshell

Anthony Zee

An intuitive, concept-driven introduction to quantum field theory. Physical insight first, formalism second — but with real equations to play with. Draw your own Feynman diagrams.

Selected chapters

Alex's pick

Indra's Pearls

Mumford, Series & Wright

The best book on fractals. Pseudocode ready for implementation, stunning visualizations, and a deep dive into the mathematics of Felix Klein's vision.

~4 months · 12 chapters

Vika's pick

The Science of Interstellar

Kip Thorne

The real physics behind the film. Black holes, relativity, time dilation — with equations and order-of-magnitude estimates. What did the movie get right?

Selected chapters

Alex's pick

The Road to Reality

Roger Penrose

A thousand-page journey through modern physics. Narrative-driven but with real problems — some famously hard. The ultimate concept map.

~9 months · 34 chapters

Vika's pick

The Black Hole War

Leonard Susskind

The debate over black hole information loss. Argue both sides — Hawking vs Susskind — then resolve using holography. Understanding through contradiction.

Full book

Alex's pick

Digestible Quantum Field Theory

Andrey Smilga

Bridges undergraduate physics and QFT without oversimplifying. A narrative exploration packed with new concepts — we'd create our own problem sets as we go.

~5 months · 17 chapters

Vika's pick

Flatland

Edwin Abbott

A geometric allegory exploring dimensions beyond our own. Translate concepts from AdS/CFT into 2D analogies. What information is lost between dimensions?
